Parts of the Guitar

The layout of a guitar is overall pretty simple. The image on the right shows a diagram of a right-handed guitar. The important parts of the guitar to remember while beginning to learn how to play it are the tuning pegs, neck, frets, and the sound hole. When you pick up the guitar, you put it on your right leg so that the finger plate (also known as a pick guard) is underneath the sound hole. The finger plate should also sit above your leg and the neck should be to your left. Your left hand will press on the frets while your right hand strums and plucks the strings in front of the sound hole. Before you play, you must first tune your guitar, which you will learn about in the next paragraph.



Tuning

It is important to tune your guitar before you play so that every string plays the right pitch. There are several different methods of tuning and most of them are simple. The easiest ways to tune are to either download a guitar tuner app on your phone, or buy a guitar tuner. There are other ways to tune such as doing it by ear and comparing your guitar's pitch to that of another instrument that is in tune. A guitar tuner will tell you whether or not you need to tighten the tunings peg to raise the pitch, or loosen it to lower the pitch. The process of tuning only takes about thirty seconds and is a good thing to get in the habit of doing. I personally recommend buying a guitar tuner, and have linked the one that I use in the image on the right. Now it's time to learn how to actually play a note!

Playing the Guitar

Making noise on the guitar is simple. Use either your hand or a guitar pick to pluck or strum the strings over the sound hole. To change the pitch of the string and play different notes, you must hold down the frets with your left hand. Place the tip of your finger on the fingerboard just before a fret. You want your finger to be touching the fret, but not directly on top of it. This will hurt your fingers at first, but will eventually stop as you start playing more. Now it's time to learn how to read guitar music!

Standard Notation vs Entablature

There are two different ways to read and write guitar music, standard notation and tabs. The image on the right shows standard notation on top and tab on the bottom. Standard notation is the way all music is normally read. It is a bit more complicated than reading tabs because it requires music theory knowledge. The tabs are a lot more simple. The six lines on the bar staff represent the six strings of the guitar, with the top string being the highest and the bottom being the lowest. The numbers represent the fret that you hold down in order to play that note.
